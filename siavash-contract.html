Engagement, Role & Scope of Work

1.1 The Company engages the Contractor as an independent contractor in the role of Director of Public Relations & Finance, effective April 23, 2026. The Contractor reports directly to Austin Neill, Founder & CEO.

1.2 This is an executive-level, dual-mandate role. The Contractor opens doors at the institutional level, synchronizes ERS's voice across every stakeholder relationship, and builds the financial architecture that turns projects into reality.

Hours & Compensation
Base Hours Minimum 15 hours per week during Phase 1. ERS is not obligated to pay for hours in excess of 15 per week in cash.

Billing & Payment The Contractor submits invoices bi-weekly. Payment is issued within 7 business days of invoice receipt.
Full-Capacity Rate The Contractor's full market rate is acknowledged by both parties to be $60.00 CAD per hour. The difference of $30.00/hr between the paid cash rate and the full market rate is banked as sweat equity credit toward the Contractor's equity buy-in as set out in Section 4.
4
Equity Structure
4.1 — Equity Grant

Upon completion of the equity buy-in obligations set out in this Section, the Contractor is entitled to a 4% equity interest in Elias Range Inc., structured across two components:

  • 3% (Component B) — earned through sweat equity (banked hours) and cash investment
  • 1% (Component C) — earned through qualifying milestone achievement

Buy-in values are fixed at the April 2026 company valuation — these values are locked regardless of any future increase in company valuation:

  • Component B (3%): $75,000 CAD
  • Component C (1%): $25,000 CAD (milestone credit cap)
  • Total 4%: $100,000 CAD
4.2 — Buy-In Components
ComponentEquityAmountMechanism

Grand Total: $100,000 = 4%

4.3 — Sweat Equity: Hour Banking

The $30.00/hr difference between the Contractor's full market rate ($60/hr) and paid cash rate ($30/hr) is banked as equity credit for all base hours worked.

  • At 15 hours/week, the accrual rate is $450/week ($30 × 15 hrs)
  • Sweat equity banking is capped at $50,000 total across the Phase 1 period
  • Accrual is tracked and reported by ERS quarterly
When the $50,000 sweat equity cap is reached: The Company's Board of Directors holds the option to begin paying the Contractor at the full cash rate of $60.00/hr if deemed appropriate given the Company's financial position. The minimum cash investment (Section 4.4) becomes due upon reaching this milestone.
4.4 — Cash Investment

The Contractor is required to invest cash directly into Elias Range Inc. to satisfy the balance of the Component B buy-in ($75,000) not covered by sweat equity.

Formula: Cash Investment = $75,000 − Sweat Equity Banked  |  Minimum: $25,000

4.5 — Milestone Credit: Component C (Additional 1%)

Upon achievement of qualifying milestones, the Contractor earns an additional $25,000 equity credit, representing an additional 1% equity interest in Elias Range Inc. (elevating total equity to the full 4% cap). This credit is capped at $25,000 and is separate from — and in addition to — the Component B buy-in.

Qualifying Milestones (any one of the following):

1. Northern Health Board & CEO Meeting
Secures and facilitates an in-person meeting between ERS and the Board of Directors and CEO of Northern Health Authority.
2. Northern Health Large Client Signed
A master lease agreement or large-scale housing contract with Northern Health is executed as a direct result of the Contractor's engagement efforts.
3. Two Large Clients Signed via Campaigns
Two significant institutional clients (health authorities, construction companies, or equivalent) are signed as a direct result of the Contractor's engagement campaigns.

Milestone achievement is determined by mutual agreement between the Contractor and Austin Neill (CEO). Upon confirmation, the 1% equity interest (Component C) is credited as fully earned.

Phase 2 — Rate Increase Conditions

The Contractor's cash rate increases from $30.00/hr to $60.00 CAD per hour at the start of Phase 2 (April 23, 2027), subject to all three of the following conditions being met:

  1. Milestone Achievement — The Contractor has achieved at least one of the qualifying milestones defined in Section 4.5
  2. Business Affordability — ERS's financial position, as assessed by the CEO, supports the increased compensation without material risk to operations
  3. Performance Review — The Contractor's 12-month performance review confirms continued alignment with role expectations and deliverables

If any condition is not met, the rate review is deferred by mutual agreement to a subsequent quarter (90-day increments) until all conditions are satisfied.

6
Deliverables & Performance

6.1 Compensation in this Agreement is explicitly tied to delivering outcomes. The Contractor is engaged as a results-driven, executive-level contributor — not on a time-in-seat basis.

6.2 The Contractor is expected to maintain active progress on the following at all times:

Public Relations & Engagement
  • At least 2 active concurrent stakeholder engagement campaigns
  • Measurable progress on Northern Health engagement track
  • Quarterly stakeholder relationship additions (new contacts, meetings, or partnerships advanced)
  • Monthly media or content activity across ERS channels
Finance
  • Active management of all open finance files (grants, lenders, investors)
  • At least one new investor or capital relationship initiated per quarter
  • All grant deadlines met with no missed submissions
  • Financial models current for all active development projects

6.3 A formal performance review is conducted at 3 months (payroll transition), 6 months, and 12 months. The 12-month review is the milestone gate for Phase 2 eligibility.

7
Independent Contractor Status

7.1 The Contractor is engaged as an independent contractor, not an employee, during Phase 1. The Contractor is responsible for all applicable taxes, source deductions, and filings arising from compensation under this Agreement during the contractor phase.

7.2 The Contractor may work for other clients during Phase 1, provided there is no conflict of interest with ERS's business. Any potential conflict must be disclosed to the CEO in advance.

7.3 Upon transition to payroll at the 3-month mark, the Contractor becomes an employee of Elias Range Inc. and all standard payroll deductions apply.

8
Confidentiality

8.1 The Contractor acknowledges that in the course of this engagement they will have access to highly confidential information including but not limited to: client identities and contracts, financial data, strategic plans, investor information, and proprietary business systems.

8.2 The Contractor agrees not to disclose any confidential information to any third party during or after the term of this Agreement, except as required in the ordinary course of performing their role for ERS.

8.3 This confidentiality obligation survives termination of this Agreement for a period of 3 years.

9
Intellectual Property

All work product, campaigns, materials, financial models, relationships, and other deliverables created by the Contractor in the course of this engagement are the sole property of Elias Range Inc. The Contractor assigns all intellectual property rights in such work to the Company upon creation.

10
Term & Termination

10.1 This Agreement commences on April 23, 2026 and continues until terminated by either party.

10.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days written notice.

10.3 The Company may terminate immediately for cause (including but not limited to: material breach, fraud, gross misconduct, or violation of confidentiality obligations). In such cases, unvested equity is forfeited.

10.4 Upon termination without cause by the Company, the Contractor retains all vested equity and receives 12 months accelerated vesting as set out in Section 4.6.

10.5 The minimum cash investment (Section 4.4) is non-refundable upon termination for cause. Upon termination without cause by the Company, the cash investment is refunded pro-rata based on unvested equity percentage.
