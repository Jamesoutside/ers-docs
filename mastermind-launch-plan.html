MVP Events
Two Events.
One Quarter.

The MVP is two events in Q2 2026 — one online, one in-person. Invite-only. No membership fee yet. Just: come, see what we're building, tell us if you want in.

Event 1 — Online
The Open Room
June 2026 · 60 minutes · Zoom · Invite-only
Austin presents: the problem, the model, what the Mastermind is
10–20 invited guests — Austin's network + warm referrals
Open Q&A — let the room tell you what they think
End: "We're building this. If you want to be a founding member, here's how."
Follow-up: waitlist link, application form
Cost: $0–$200 (Zoom + prep)
Event 2 — In Person
The Founding Dinner
July/August 2026 · Vancouver · Private dining room · 10–15 people
Small, tight, no frills — just the right people at a good table
Austin runs it personally — no event planner, no back-of-house team
Structured agenda: problem → model → what we're asking people to commit to
2–3 "testers" invited to experience the format and give feedback
Gate: do people leave wanting to come back? Do they bring someone next time?
Cost: $1,500–$3,000 (venue + food, ~$150–$200/head)
Member Attraction
Inside Your Network.
Outside It Too.

Austin's existing network gets the Mastermind started. These channels grow it beyond people you already know.

🤝 Direct Outreach

Austin personally reaches out to 15–20 people he knows — healthcare contacts, investors, builders, lawyers. Personal message, not a mass email. "I'm building something — I want you in the room."

Effort: Low · Cost: $0 · Closes fastest

🔗 Warm Referrals

Every founding member brings one person they know. "Who do you know that should be in this room?" Compound quickly. 10 founding members = 10 warm introductions = exponential quality network.

Effort: Low · Cost: $0 · High quality leads

💼 LinkedIn Campaign

Austin posts about the Mastermind — the problem, the mission, what's being built. Not a pitch. A story. 3–4 posts over the quarter. Targeted to healthcare, construction, real estate, and BC government audiences.

Effort: Medium · Cost: $0–$500 (ads optional)

🎙️ Event Circuit

Austin is already at Build Deck, VRCA, Canadian Institute events. At each one: mention the Mastermind in conversation. Collect cards. Follow up with the waitlist link. Your existing event presence IS the marketing.

Effort: Medium · Cost: $0 (event tickets already paid)

📧 ERS Email List

ERS already has relationships with hospitals, health authorities, and construction contacts. A targeted email to the most relevant contacts — not a blast, a curated send — introduces the Mastermind to people who already trust ERS.

Effort: Low · Cost: $0 · High trust baseline

🎓 Healthcare Events

VCH forums, Northern Health stakeholder events, Interior Health capital consultations. These are where the VP-level healthcare decision-makers are. Position the Mastermind as a solution-building group — not a pitch, a partnership.

Effort: High · Cost: low · Highest value audience
Decision Gates
When to Go.
When to Wait.

Each phase has a gate. Don't move to the next phase until the gate is passed.

Phase 1
Now → Q2
Gate to pass
5+ people have said "I want to be part of this" after seeing the concept. Team (Megan) has the admin stack set up. Legal template done. Waitlist live.
If gate not passed
Keep building the outreach. Refine the story. Don't run the MVP event until you have warm bodies to fill the room.
Phase 2
Q2 → Q3
Gate to pass
After MVP events: 3+ people say "I'll pay to be a member." Feedback is positive. The format works. You know who your founding member ICP is.
If gate not passed
Run one more MVP iteration. Change the format or the audience before committing to the full launch infrastructure.
Phase 3
Q3 Launch
Gate to pass
10+ committed founding members. Revenue projection covers the year. Event planner contracted. First full event booked. This is the moment you open the doors properly.
What launch looks like
First mandatory in-person event (Vancouver Club or equivalent). Full calendar announced. Founding membership roster published. The Mastermind is real.
