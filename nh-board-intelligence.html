Executive Summary

Five Things That Jump Off the Page

The most important intelligence from 31 NH board meetings across 5 years — distilled to what ERS needs to know before any campaign conversation.

1

Housing Is an Official NH Strategic Initiative — And They Still Haven't Solved It

The "Housing Prototype Program" appears in every board meeting from October 2022 onward. By October 2025, "Increasing Community Housing and Childcare for Health Care Staff" is explicitly listed as a board-level strategic priority. NH isn't passively aware of the housing problem — they've been actively trying to solve it for 3+ years and haven't succeeded. ERS walks in as the answer to a question they've already publicly articulated.

2

The Dawson Creek Hospital Has Been "In Progress" for 5+ Years — and It's Opening Now

DCDH Hospital Replacement ($377M → $589.61M) appears in every single capital report from 2021 through 2026. The building was sealed and closed in late 2024. The new hospital is 50% larger than the old one. This is an active opening window — the time to establish housing infrastructure is now, before the opening completes.

3

GoHealth BC Is Not a Competitor — ERS Houses Their Nurses

GoHealth BC is NH's internal travel nurse staffing program (rebranded from Travel Resource Program in 2023). GoHealth nurses explicitly fill a 10% vacancy gap alongside agency nurses. Monthly nursing hours doubled from ~14,000 to ~29,700 in just 12 months (2023–2024). These are travelling nurses who need housing at their deployment sites. ERS houses GoHealth nurses and permanently placed staff alike.

4

The Deficit Spiral Is Real, Worsening, and ERS Directly Addresses the Root Cause

NH went from near-zero deficit in 2021–22 to a $39.2 million operating deficit by FY2024-25. Agency staff and overtime to fill vacancies is explicitly cited as the primary cost driver. The cycle is clear: vacancies → agency nurses → overtime rates → budget overage → deficit. ERS breaks this cycle by making NH communities livable — reducing reliance on expensive agency deployments.

5

Workforce Crisis Is the #1 Ongoing Board Issue — Language Has Escalated to "Crisis"

Recruitment and retention appears in all 31 documents across 5 years. Vacancy rates hit 20–60% in rural nursing by late 2022. The language escalates from "challenging" (2021) → quantified (2022) → new programs launched (2023) → housing elevated to board strategic priority (2025). As of April 2025, the overall vacancy rate remains above 20%. The structural problem is not solved — ERS arrives with a scalable answer.

⭐ Bottom Line

NH has been publicly searching for a full-service housing operator since April 2023. In the board's own words: "Explore full-service providers to operate housing units on NH's behalf in select areas." That is ERS's exact model. The prototype exists. It's not scaled. ERS is the execution of their stated strategy.

2021

Baseline and Emerging Crisis

Meetings: Feb 8, Apr 12, Jun 14, Oct 20, Dec 6

  • Financial: Near-zero deficit through most of 2021. Surplus of $2.7M at Period 11. December 2021: first real deficit at –$1.7M.
  • Capital: DCDH Hospital Replacement listed as "In Progress" in every capital plan. Pre-2021 project.
  • Workforce: Staffing challenges framed as pandemic-related. Locum physician coverage. IEN discussions begin.
  • Housing: No specific housing program yet — "accommodation infrastructure" discussion begins.
  • Key themes: COVID-19 dominant. System recovery. Vaccination rollouts. Financial stability with Ministry supplemental funding.
2022
Crisis Named

The Workforce Crisis Named — Housing Program Launched

Meetings: Feb 7, Apr 11, Jun 13, Oct 18, Dec 5 (+ minutes)

  • Financial: Dec 2022: –$3.8M operating deficit. Agency/overtime for LTC vacancies primary driver.
  • Capital: DCDH confirmed at $377.86M In Progress. Total NH capital: $411.4M approved. CEO signing authority increased to $2.05M.
  • Workforce: Overall vacancy 20.31%. Rural nursing vacancy 20–60%. RN demand 3× supply since 2017. 55% of departures within first 3 years.
  • Housing: 🟢 "Housing Prototype Program" officially listed as active NH HR initiative — first appearance Oct 2022. Paired with Childcare Prototype Program.
  • IEN meeting in Dawson Creek (Nov 2022): 20 internationally educated nurses — housing and childcare identified as settlement barriers.

⭐ Key moment

The Housing Prototype Program is born. This is the foundation of everything ERS builds on.

2023
GoHealth Born

GoHealth Born, Deficit Spikes, Full-Service Provider Ask

Meetings: Feb 13, Apr 17, Jun 11, Oct 18, Dec 11

  • Financial: Apr 2023: –$18.5M full-year deficit — significant escalation. Dec heading toward –$14.4M.
  • Capital: DCDH continues. New DC hospital eligible for $625K CleanBC incentive. Three hospitals (Terrace, Fort St. James, Dawson Creek) listed as strategic portfolio.
  • GoHealth: Travel Resource Program rebranded "GoHealth BC" (spring 2023). Active employees tripled in 24 months. 71 nurses joined in one 12-month period. Serving 14 NH communities.
  • Housing Prototype Sites confirmed: Kitimat, Hazelton, Prince Rupert, Chetwynd, Dawson Creek, Fort St. John, Robson Valley. NW: 184 beds | NI: 56 beds | NE: 108 beds.
  • Critical ask: Board explicitly: "Explore full-service providers to operate housing units on NH's behalf."
  • Vacancy: 20.71% overall. 4,747 positions posted; 10–13% "difficult to fill" (active 90+ days).

⚠ Critical finding

NH asked for a full-service housing operator in April 2023. Three years later, the position still hasn't been filled. ERS is the answer.

2024
DC 50% Done

Dawson Creek Hospital 50% Complete — GoHealth Doubles

Meetings: Feb 12, Apr 15, Jun 24, Dec 9

  • Capital: DCDH updated to $433.6M approved. Board made site visit to Dawson Creek construction (June 2024). Building sealed and closed by year-end.
  • GoHealth growth: Monthly nursing hours: Oct 2023 ~14,000 → Sep 2024 ~29,700 (doubled in 12 months). Agency hours also surging: 36K → 55K/month.
  • Workforce: Fort St. John — 10% vacancy gap "supported by GoHealth and agency nurses." NH building 300+ locum contacts. Communities establishing housing/childcare partnership tables.
  • PRRI data: Dawson Creek has 477 employees receiving the Provincial Rural Retention Incentive ($8,000/yr).
  • GoHealth OHS program won 2024 Leading Award. ED upskilling program initiated.
2025
Crisis Peak

$39M Deficit — Housing Elevated to Board Strategic Priority

Meetings: Feb 10, Apr 14, Jun 17, Oct 22, Dec 9

  • Financial: –$39.2M operating deficit (FY2024-25 full year) — largest in the 5-year record. Primary drivers: acute care overtime + agency, LTC care aide vacancies, MOH contribution shortfall.
  • Capital: DCDH budget updated to $589.61M (56% increase from original). LEED Gold construction. Over 50% larger than old hospital, 25% less total GHG. ARC Resources as lead donor for $3M MRI campaign.
  • Oct 2025: 🟢 "Increasing Community Housing and Childcare for Health Care Staff" listed as a formal NH Board strategic priority. Escalation: program (2022) → prototype (2023) → strategic priority (2025).
  • Fort James hospital opened Jan 14, 2025 — new hospital 3× size, 27 beds, expanded ED. This is the model for DC opening.
  • Vacancy: Apr 2025 = 20.06%. Still above 20% after 3 years of programs.

⚠ Most significant year

2025 is the culmination: largest deficit, housing becomes a board-level strategic priority, DC hospital in final pre-opening phase. The urgency window is open now.
