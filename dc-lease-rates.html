The Number That Works for Both Sides

This document establishes the lease rate range for the 32 furnished residential units, grounded in: what Northern Health currently pays in hotels, what produces strong cash flow and a defensible completed valuation, and what position to enter negotiations from versus where to settle.

The Market Benchmark — What NH Pays Today

Northern Health currently accommodates itinerant staff, locum physicians, and travel nurses in hotel rooms and substandard private rentals. This is the rate Elias Range Stays must price against — not the open rental market.

The ERS product is superior to hotel in every measurable dimension — fully furnished, professional-grade, purpose-built, single-invoice billing, 24/7 management support, and a managed residential environment rather than a transient hotel. The question is how much of that value we capture vs. how much we pass to NH as savings.

ERS Value Proposition to NH: At $4,800/month, Northern Health saves $1,200/unit/month versus a $200/night hotel, while receiving a materially better product and experience for their staff. Across 32 units at 90% occupancy, that's $414,720 in annual savings for Northern Health — while ERS earns strong cash flow.

The Pitch to Northern Health

Frame It as Their Win

Do not present this as a lease negotiation. Present it as a healthcare recruitment and retention solution that Northern Health has been unable to self-supply — and that happens to cost them less than the hotel model they currently rely on.

"At $4,800/month per unit, Northern Health saves over $414,000 per year compared to current hotel costs — while providing their staff with a superior, purpose-built, professionally managed environment that increases retention. This is not a housing cost. It is a workforce strategy."

The three-point pitch:

  • Cost: 20–25% below the hotel rate for a better product
  • Retention: Staff who are well-housed stay longer — reducing Northern Health's recruitment and onboarding costs
  • Simplicity: One invoice. No per-employee expense management. No hotel booking logistics.
