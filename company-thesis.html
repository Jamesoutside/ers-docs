Section 01 — The Problem

Healthcare is failing because the people who deliver it have nowhere to live.

20.3% Nursing vacancy rate, Northern Health
$39.2M NH operating deficit FY2024–25
2–3× Agency nurse cost vs. permanent hire

BC's healthcare system is in crisis — not because of funding, not because of equipment, not because of policy. It's because the nurses, doctors, locums, and allied health professionals who keep hospitals running can't find stable, affordable housing near the communities they serve.

The numbers are documented and worsening:

  • Northern Health: 20.3% nursing vacancy rate. $39.2M operating deficit in FY2024-25, driven primarily by agency staff costs and overtime.
  • 25 service interruptions in Chetwynd in 2024 alone.
  • 900 new staff recruited in 2024 — still not enough to close the gap.
  • Agency nurses cost 2–3× a permanent hire. Travel staff in hotels = isolation, burnout, and turnover.
  • The BC government has committed $2.95B to worker housing — explicitly naming health authorities as eligible partners.

This is not a local problem. It is a systemic infrastructure failure. And it has a clear, addressable root cause: there is no purpose-built, professionally managed housing infrastructure for healthcare workers in rural and remote BC.

The hotel is not a home. The empty apartment is not a community. The rotating locum is not a retained employee.

Section 02 — The Thesis

Housing is healthcare infrastructure. ERS builds it.

ERS's thesis is simple: stable, community-connected housing for healthcare workers is not a nice-to-have. It is a precondition for a functioning healthcare system. Every hospital in rural BC depends on it. Every health authority is struggling without it.

ERS was founded on the belief that this gap is not just a social problem — it is a massive, underserved infrastructure opportunity. One that requires a dedicated company, a purpose-built model, and a long-term commitment to the communities it serves.

We are not a property manager. We are not a landlord. We are not a hotel chain. We are a healthcare housing infrastructure company — the first of its kind in BC, and potentially in Canada.

Section 03 — What We Do

End-to-end. From raw land to fully operational community.

ERS provides the complete stack of healthcare workforce housing:

Operate

We manage existing units — furnished, cleaned, maintained, booked, and supported 24/7. One invoice, zero admin burden for the health authority.

Acquire

We source and take over existing housing portfolios from health authorities and landlords. We acquire ownership, hold the assets, and run the books.

Plan

We work with health authorities to map regional housing need — beds, staff profiles, communities, program scope. We turn their data into an infrastructure plan.

Build

We acquire land, secure permits, and develop purpose-designed healthcare housing hub facilities — clinical spaces, community programming, daycare, recreational amenities, concierge service, fresh food programs.

Convene

We bring together municipalities, national organizations (YMCA), local businesses, and adjacent healthcare professionals to activate the full community platform around every facility.

Consult

We advise health authorities on the transition to purpose-built housing infrastructure — before, during, and after deployment.

Scale: We deploy this model across BC and Canada — one community at a time, with a repeatable playbook that gets better with every project.

Section 04 — Why Now

The window is open. It won't stay open forever.

Three forces are converging right now that make this the right moment:

  1. The deficit is forcing action.

    Northern Health's $39.2M deficit is primarily a staffing cost problem. Staffing cost is primarily a housing problem. Health authorities can no longer absorb the cost of agency nurses in hotels. They need a permanent solution.

  2. The policy is in place.

    BC Builds ($2.95B), GoHealth BC, and Infrastructure BC have created a policy and funding environment that makes ERS-style partnerships not just possible, but actively sought. Health authorities are looking for exactly what ERS provides.

  3. The hospital construction wave is here.

    Dawson Creek ($377M), Burnaby, Surrey, Cowichan — a wave of new hospital builds is creating immediate demand for workforce housing for both construction crews and incoming clinical staff. ERS is positioned at the front of this wave.

The window to become the infrastructure partner of choice for BC's health authorities is open right now. In five years, someone will own this space. ERS intends to be that company.

Section 05 — The Model

More commitment. More value. Lower cost.

ERS operates on a tiered commitment model:

Pilot

Health authority tests ERS in one community. Immediate cost savings vs. hotels and agency housing.

Regional

Multi-community master lease. ERS becomes the regional housing partner. Community programming, HR reporting, retained staff outcomes.

Infrastructure Partnership

Health authority invests alongside ERS. Purpose-built facility, lowest cost per unit, full platform: clinical spaces, daycare, fresh food, events, concierge, executive reviews.

The deeper the commitment, the more the health authority gets — and the less it costs per unit. This is a model designed to create long-term, deepening partnerships, not transactional contracts.

Section 06 — The Opportunity

A national infrastructure company, starting in BC.

The addressable market is enormous:

  • 5 health authorities in BC. 18 Northern Health hospitals alone.
  • A national wave of rural healthcare staffing crises mirroring BC's.
  • Construction and infrastructure workforce housing across Western Canada.
  • The same model applies to every remote hospital, every major construction project, every government-employed workforce that needs stable housing.

ERS is starting in BC — specifically Northern BC, where the need is most acute, the relationships are warmest, and the proof points are building. But the vision is national.

The company we are building is not a property management company. It is a healthcare housing infrastructure company — purpose-built, mission-driven, and designed to become an essential partner of every health authority in Canada.
