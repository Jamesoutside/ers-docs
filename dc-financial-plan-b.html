Plan B — Grant-Funded Alternative

Project Financial Plan:
NRCan OSCC Grants + LVCU for Remainder — No Investors

This alternative financial plan models the project assuming NRCan OSCC Phase 1 ($180,000) and Phase 2 ($10,000,000) grants are awarded, along with the South Peace Mackenzie Trust contribution ($250,000). Under this scenario, investor equity is not required and the remainder is funded by LVCU. This plan is presented as a template — actual figures depend on grant award amounts and terms.

Plan B — Core Premise

What Changes When Grants Replace Investors

Plan A — With Investors
• $5,318,600 equity from Fulmer & Co. (35% stake)
• Investors receive 35% of project equity
• $9,877,400 LVCU construction mortgage
• Grants supplementary / reduce debt
• Total: $15,196,000
Plan B — Grants + LVCU Only
• $10,430,000 in grants (OSCC Ph1 + Ph2 + SPMTrust)
• No investor equity — 100% ownership retained
• LVCU funds the remaining $4,766,000
• Significantly lower debt service
• Total: $15,196,000
Key Benefit of Plan B: Elias Range Development retains 100% equity ownership and avoids the 35% dilution to investors. The NRCan OSCC Phase 2 grant ($10M) essentially replaces the investor capital. The tradeoff is dependency on grant approvals — which are not guaranteed.

Section 5 — Recommendation

How to Use Both Plans

Plan A (Investor + LVCU) is the primary financing strategy — it is executable without grant approval, uses the confirmed LVCU Discussion Paper terms, and allows the project to proceed regardless of OSCC outcome. The 35% equity dilution is the cost of certainty.

Plan B (Grants + LVCU) is the preferred outcome — it preserves 100% ownership, generates dramatically better cash flow, and requires a much smaller LVCU facility. It should be pursued in parallel with Plan A. If the NRCan OSCC Phase 2 grant is awarded, the investor component is retired and Plan B becomes the active structure.

For the South Peace Mackenzie Trust: The $250,000 contribution is applicable under either plan. Under Plan A it reduces the LVCU requirement marginally. Under Plan B it contributes to the grant stack that replaces investor equity entirely.
