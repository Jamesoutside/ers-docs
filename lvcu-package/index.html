The Investment Thesis

Three Reasons This Financing Is Sound

Pillar 1 — Documented Demand

The market gap is real, sourced, and active today

A $378M hospital replacement is under construction. 50% of workers need housing. Zero vacancy rates have been documented in Dawson Creek since 2008. Northern Health has formally identified the housing shortage as their #1 operational barrier. This is not a speculative demand — it exists right now.

Pillar 2 — Rising Property Value

The land appreciates at every stage of development

At development permit and demolition of the current structure, the appraised value of 10620 10th Street will exceed its current appraisal. A cleared, zoned, permitted commercial lot in a supply-constrained northern market carries a materially higher value than the as-is property with an aging structure. The loan is secured by an asset that grows.

Pillar 3 — Institutional Coalition

The tenancy is backed by institutions, not individuals

Northern Health, the City of Dawson Creek, and YMCA BC have all provided formal letters of support. NRCan has received a Phase 1 OSCC grant application. The occupancy pipeline is institutional — healthcare workers placed by a health authority, not walk-in retail tenants. This is the lowest-risk tenant profile possible for a residential-commercial asset.
