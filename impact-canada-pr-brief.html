1
Who We Are

Elias Range Stays (ERS) is a workforce housing company that solves one of Canada's most pressing infrastructure problems: healthcare and construction workers who travel to underserved communities cannot find suitable, affordable housing — and that gap is costing hospitals, health authorities, and general contractors billions of dollars in turnover, vacancies, and project delays.

ERS provides fully managed, institutional-quality housing for travelling workers — at roughly $105/night versus the $200+ hotel alternative — through a single-invoice, 24/7 support model purpose-built for healthcare and construction organizations.

Where We Are Today
  • 34+ units under management across Northern BC
  • 6 hospitals served — active health authority relationships
  • ~40% cost savings vs. hotel accommodation for institutional clients
  • 4 years incorporated, 2 years of real growth — now scaling aggressively
  • Active development pipeline: Dawson Creek Healthcare Hub, UHNBC Prince George, and an Ontario hospital project

We are at a pivotal moment. The business works. The model is proven. The demand is clear. What we need now is a coordinated, professional PR and stakeholder engagement strategy that opens doors at the institutional level and positions ERS as the preferred workforce housing partner across BC and Canada.

2
The PR Mandate

We are looking for a PR partner who can build a unified, coordinated engagement strategy so that all of our stakeholder categories hear the same story and are guided toward the same outcome: ERS as the trusted, preferred workforce housing partner in BC and across Canada.

This is not a communications retainer or a social media management role. This is executive-level strategic PR — the kind of work that opens boardroom doors, generates institutional credibility, and turns stakeholder relationships into signed agreements. We need someone who can operate at that level.

The Core PR Problem We're Solving

ERS operates at the intersection of healthcare, construction, housing, and government — four worlds with different cultures, different decision-makers, and different languages. We need a single, coherent narrative that lands powerfully in all four — and a PR strategy that knows which door to knock on first, and how.

3
Stakeholder Map

These are the eight stakeholder categories we need active engagement with. Some are further along than others. A strong PR partner will assess where we are in each and build the engagement track accordingly.

Health Authorities
Northern Health, Interior Health, VCH, Fraser Health, Island Health. Primary goal: master lease agreements and institutional housing partnerships.
Government & Infrastructure
Infrastructure BC (UHNBC Prince George), BC Housing, provincial ministries, CMHC, NRCan. Goal: preferred partner designation and program alignment.
Healthcare Unions & Associations
Nurses unions, CUPE health divisions, Doctors of BC, CMA, BCCNR. Housing-as-retention narrative. Referral and advocacy pipelines.
Construction Unions & Labour
BC Building Trades, local union halls, PLA stakeholders. Goal: ERS housing embedded as a standard Project Labour Agreement benefit for remote-site workers.
Industry Associations
VRCA, BCCA, BCCR, Urban Land Institute, real estate development associations. Credibility with GCs, project owners, and institutional capital.
First Nations & Indigenous Communities
Treaty Nations, Band Councils, economic development arms. Equity development partnerships. Target: 2–3 active First Nations partnerships in Northern BC.
Municipalities
Prince George, Dawson Creek, Terrace, Prince Rupert, Squamish. Mayors, economic development, and planning departments. Goal: community recognition and local champion relationships.
Media
Digital (LinkedIn, Facebook, ERS social channels), print (trade publications, white papers, press releases), and broadcast (earned media, Austin Neill speaking appearances).
4
Campaign Priorities

We envision five concurrent engagement campaigns running simultaneously. A skilled PR partner will know how to resource and sequence these to create momentum without diluting focus:

  1. Healthcare Worker Housing Campaign — health authorities and healthcare unions running in parallel, with a shared housing-as-retention narrative
  2. Construction Workforce Campaign — targeting union leadership and general contractors, positioning ERS as the standard for remote crew housing
  3. First Nations Development Campaign — equity partnerships, shared outcomes, and co-development opportunities in Northern BC
  4. Investor & Partner Awareness Campaign — building ERS's profile among private equity, mission-aligned capital, and strategic partners
  5. Municipal & Government Campaign — city councils, economic development offices, and provincial stakeholders recognizing ERS as a community infrastructure partner

What Success Looks Like

Within the first 12 months of engagement, we would consider this partnership successful if:

  • A meeting is secured with the Board of Directors and/or CEO of Northern Health Authority
  • At least one master lease agreement or large institutional client is signed as a result of engagement efforts
  • ERS is featured in 3+ trade publications or earned media placements
  • Austin Neill is on the speaking circuit at 2+ relevant industry events
  • Active, warm relationships exist at the decision-maker level with at least 3 health authorities
  • Two or more of the five campaigns have moved from initiation to active momentum
