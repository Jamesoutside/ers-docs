2
Draw Close Program — Cowichan Bay Hospital Project
Tabled Item · Budget Approval
Discussion Point

Austin proposes running a Draw Close appreciation event/program for the Cowichan Bay Hospital project. Budget approximately $4,500, initially coming from ERS's bottom line and priced into future contracts going forward. The program builds genuine community on the construction side — ERS takes care of workers not just in housing but in community, celebrating their contribution at project close. It's a client value-add, a staff appreciation event, and a powerful organic marketing tool as workers talk to other crews and contractors across the industry.

Detail
  • Estimated event/program budget: ~$4,500
  • Target: Cowichan Bay hospital project — client companies and all their staff
  • Purpose 1 — Client value-add: Adds real, tangible value to the contractor relationship beyond housing. Demonstrates ERS is integrated into the wellbeing of their workforce.
  • Purpose 2 — Community building: Creates a genuine celebration of the crew and their work. Builds loyalty and appreciation between ERS and the teams we house.
  • Purpose 3 — Organic marketing: Workers tell other workers. Contractors tell other contractors. "Our housing provider threw us a party and covered everything" is word-of-mouth that no ad budget can buy.
  • Pricing going forward: Costs absorbed on first contract; built into future pricing. Example: at $120/night rate, price to $122 — $2/night accumulates over a 2-year contract to fully fund the program at project close.
  • Duration: runs through the end of the Cowichan Bay hospital project
  • Planned for Q3 2026
  • Budget owner and program manager to be confirmed (see Item 3)
