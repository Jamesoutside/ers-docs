1

About This Role

Laurie's role is to operate and organize the company so Austin can lead it. She brings structure, governance, and operational rigor — building the systems, managing the people, running the financials, and ensuring every part of the organization is moving with clarity and accountability. This frees Austin to focus on leadership, strategy, business development, partnerships, and the relationships that grow the company.

2

Core Responsibilities

Build and maintain the company P&L, annual budget, and 5-year financial plan
Manage and direct all day-to-day operations across the organization
Build and implement organizational structure — procedures, SOPs, accountability frameworks
People management: level up each team member, assign clear outcomes, hold people accountable
File management, scheduling, timelines, and meeting cadence
Support hiring decisions and onboarding of new team members
Facilitate regular, productive team meetings with clear agendas, notes, and resolutions
Build corporate governance processes in line with the company's entity structure
Track and report key business metrics and performance indicators
Manage projects and unit scaling — ensuring operational capacity keeps pace with growth
Coordinate across all company pillars: Housing Operations, Development, Finance, Marketing/PR

6

Working With Austin — How This Works Best

Do

  • Come prepared — read the room, know what's happening before the meeting starts
  • Bring summaries — Austin thinks in outcomes, not updates
  • Deliver key outcomes with clear deadlines — he's intense, match that energy
  • Take good notes — write it down, act on it, close the loop
  • Bring resolutions — don't surface problems without a proposed solution
  • Keep everyone clear on what they need to do — direction, accountability, deadlines
  • Organize everything — files, people, timelines, schedules should always be findable
  • Be direct — Austin doesn't want to guess what you're thinking

Don't

  • Don't come unprepared to meetings
  • Don't create more noise — simplify, don't complicate
  • Don't let things drift without flagging it
