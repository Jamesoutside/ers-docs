Why This Matters to You

What Federal Action Here Achieves for Peace River–Westlock

The healthcare staffing crisis in Dawson Creek is not new — but the solution is. For the first time, a community-built, federally-eligible proposal exists to solve it. What happens next will define the healthcare story in Peace Country for the next generation — and the MP who champions it will be the reason it happened.

The issues your constituents raise most consistently are healthcare access and the cost of living in the North. This initiative addresses both directly. When Elias Range Development's modular housing hub is built:

  • Dawson Creek's emergency department stabilises — fewer closures, more consistent care
  • Physicians and nurses choose to stay — and word travels to colleagues
  • Northern Health redirects millions in locum spending to direct patient care
  • A replicable model is created for every underserved northern community in your region

This is not a pilot study or a framework. It is a shovel-ready project with land owned, partners signed, and a federal application submitted.

The Broader Picture

What This Means for Northern BC — and for Canada

Dawson Creek is the proof of concept. The same model — modular, fast-deploy, institutionally-backed — is replicable across every underserved Northern Health community: Fort St. John, Smithers, Terrace, Prince George. The federal investment that proves the model in Dawson Creek seeds the solution across the entire northern corridor.

"Northern Health identifies Dawson Creek as one of five priority anchor communities for workforce housing — alongside Fort St. John, Smithers, Terrace, and Prince George. Dawson Creek is where this starts."

— Northern Health Workforce Housing & Services Partnership Framework

Canada's Canada Health Act guarantees every citizen comparable access to healthcare regardless of geography. In Peace Country, that guarantee is not being kept. The MP who changes that — with a concrete, funded, operational solution — delivers something that no amount of framework documents or consultation processes can.

This is the opportunity. The coalition is in place. The application is submitted. What is needed now is a champion in Ottawa.
