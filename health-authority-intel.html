Northern Health

Northern Health — Deep Profile

Northern Health is the most active, most vocal, and most documented case of healthcare housing shortage in BC. They are actively seeking housing partners right now.

Active Housing RFP — Prince Rupert (February 2024)

Northern Health posted a BC Bid RFP seeking private landlords to house healthcare staff in Prince Rupert. Market-rate budget. Start date: May 1, 2024 (negotiable earlier). Initial 1-year lease, renewable indefinitely. This is a direct, documented procurement opportunity for ERS.

Source: North Coast Review, February 7, 2024 — northcoastreview.blogspot.com
900 Net New Staff in 2024 — Still Not Enough

Northern Health recruited 900 net new employees in 2024, including 450 nurses. Despite this, chronic service disruptions continue: Chetwynd had 25 service interruptions, Dawson Creek had double-digit disruptions, and Fort St. John ER closed 5 times in a single week (July 12–18, 2024). $30,000 signing incentives plus retention bonuses are in place.

Source: CJDC-TV / BC Rural Health Network, January 24, 2025 — bcruralhealth.org
Incoming Nurse-to-Patient Ratios Will Require Even More Staff

BC's new mandatory minimum nurse-to-patient ratio policy (1:4 in medical/surgical, 24/7) will force Northern Health to recruit a "large influx" of additional healthcare workers beyond current targets — increasing housing demand significantly.

Source: Lisa Zanatta, VP Clinical Operations, Northern Health, January 2025
Northern Health Maintains Regional Housing Portfolio

NH maintains housing across the entire Northern BC region for both temporary and permanent staff placements. Stated intent: "ensure health care staff have a safe, comfortable, and modern accommodation experience after providing health care services to the community."

Source: North Coast Review, February 2024 (citing Northern Health)
GoHealth BC — Northern Health is the Operating Authority

BC's provincial travel nurse program is operated through Northern Health, with Interior Health, Island Health, the Ministry of Health, BCNU, and HSA as partners. All accommodation is provided as standard compensation (typically with kitchenette). Housing is the #1 recruitment tool.

Source: gohealthbc.ca

“Northern Health maintains a range of housing across the region for health care staff that are working in the region either on a temporary basis or are moving to the community and require temporary housing. The intent of providing housing is to ensure health care staff have a safe, comfortable, and modern accommodation experience after providing health care services to the community.”

— Northern Health, via North Coast Review, February 2024

“Housing challenges have been identified in the past as one of the key challenges towards recruitment for Health Care in the region.”

— North Coast Review, February 7, 2024

“That will mean we need to recruit even more nurses because this is going to bring a large influx of health care workers into our system.”

— Lisa Zanatta, VP Clinical Operations, Northern Health, January 2025

“We are much more successful when we have local people with local roots, families. Their connection points in rural areas get the training because they’re more likely to stay in a rural area.”

— Lisa Zanatta, VP Clinical Operations, Northern Health, January 2025
Interior Health

Interior Health

Interior Health faces the same structural conditions as Northern Health — rural communities, housing affordability challenges, nurse-to-patient ratio mandates incoming — but has been less publicly vocal about housing specifically.

GoHealth BC Partner — Housing Provided to All Travel Nurses

Interior Health is a GoHealth BC partner. All accommodation is provided to travel nurses deployed to Interior Health communities, confirming housing is part of the standard rural staffing equation. Upcoming minimum nurse-to-patient ratio (mNPR) policy will require significant additional nursing workforce across Interior communities.

Source: gohealthbc.ca
No Direct Housing RFP Found — Potential Gap to Fill

No public Interior Health housing RFP was found in this research window. This may represent an opportunity: ERS could be first to proactively offer a housing solution before IH reaches the point of public procurement.

Research gap — no public communications found on housing procurement
Island Health

Island Health (VIHA)

GoHealth BC Partner — Active Travel Nurse Deployment

Island Health is a GoHealth BC partner. Travel nurses are deployed to rural Vancouver Island communities including Port Hardy, northern Vancouver Island, and Gulf Islands with all accommodation and transportation provided.

Source: gohealthbc.ca

The new Cowichan District Hospital replacement (Duncan, BC) is under construction with ~20% of workers expected to need temporary housing. This is a construction-side opportunity for ERS concurrent with any Island Health staff housing partnership.

Source: Infrastructure BC / BC Government
No Direct Housing RFP Found

Fraser Health & VCH

Fraser Health & Vancouver Coastal Health

Hospital Construction Wave — Construction-Side Opportunity

Surrey Hospital ($2.88B), Burnaby Hospital Phase 2 ($1.7B), and Richmond Hospital ($1.95B) are all under construction in these authorities' service areas. The primary ERS opportunity here is construction worker housing, not staff housing — Metro Vancouver's large labor pool reduces the rural housing shortage dynamic.

Source: Infrastructure BC, Canada Construction Connect
No Direct Housing RFPs Found — Lower Priority for ERS

No public housing RFPs from Fraser Health or VCH found in this research window. Metro Vancouver's housing market and larger local labor pool make these authorities lower priority for ERS vs. Northern BC.

Research gap — focus resources on Northern Health first
National Patterns

National Health Authority Patterns

New Brunswick — Vitalité & Horizon: Travel Nurse Spending Scandal

CBC New Brunswick documented Vitalité and Horizon Health Networks spending tens of millions on agency and travel nurses in 2023. Health Minister publicly called the costs "exorbitant." This pattern — reactive, expensive, ad-hoc staffing solutions — is what ERS's model is designed to replace. NB's crisis is BC's near-future without structural housing solutions.

Source: CBC New Brunswick, 2023 (cbc.ca/news/canada/new-brunswick)
BC Care Providers Association — Years of Advocacy on Housing

The BCCPA has "continuously urged the government to work with BC Housing in building, purchasing, or leasing new worker housing in communities facing acute worker shortages." The February 2024 BC Builds announcement was a direct response to this advocacy.

Source: Terry Lake, CEO, BCCPA — bccare.ca, February 2024
Rural Physician Shortage — Housing as Documented Barrier Nationally

Across Ontario, Atlantic Canada, and BC, rural physician recruitment is consistently impeded by housing availability. Northern Health's $30,000 signing bonus program is one response; BC Builds is another. The problem is structural and multi-provincial.

Source: Multiple provincial health authorities, 2022–2025
Government Announcements

Government Announcements — Housing & Healthcare

BC Builds — $2.95B Worker Housing Commitment (February 2024)

$2B in low-cost financing plus $950M committed. Health authorities explicitly named as eligible "community landlords." BC Care Providers Association confirmed this directly addresses the healthcare staffing housing crisis. This is the policy framework that makes an ERS-HA partnership easy to fund and approve.

Source: BC Government news release HOUS0006-000188; BC Care Providers Association — bccare.ca
GoHealth BC — Provincial Travel Nurse Program

Government-funded travel nurse program operated through Northern Health. All accommodation provided as standard compensation. Partners: Northern Health, Interior Health, Island Health, Ministry of Health, BCNU, HSA. Expanding to additional communities. Direct model for what ERS can provide at scale.

Source: BC Ministry of Health / gohealthbc.ca
Synthesis

What Health Authorities Actually Need

Problems Publicly Acknowledged

Housing shortage = #1 recruitment barrier. Chronic service disruptions from understaffing. Reactive, expensive hotel/agency solutions. Internal housing programs overwhelmed. New nurse-to-patient ratios will dramatically increase demand.

Solutions They've Tried (and their limits)

GoHealth BC travel nurse program (housing included, but limited scale). In-house housing portfolios (limited supply, admin burden). $30K signing bonuses (expensive, doesn't solve housing). Hotel blocks (costly, no community, staff don't return). BC Builds (good policy framework, but needs private sector operators).

The Gap ERS Fills

A private-sector operator that runs the housing so health authorities don't have to. BC Builds-aligned, so financially approvable. Grassroots and community-connected, so staff actually want to stay. Single invoice. Standardised operations. Scalable across all NH communities without NH carrying the capital or the admin burden.
