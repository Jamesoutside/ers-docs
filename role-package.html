Overview — The Position & Where It's Going

What This Position Is

The Director of Public Relations & Finance is an executive-level, dual-mandate role at Elias Range Stays. It exists at the intersection of two things: how we show up in the world, and how we fund what we're building.

This is not a communications coordinator or a bookkeeper. This is the person who opens doors at the institutional level, synchronizes ERS's voice across every channel and stakeholder relationship, and builds the financial architecture that turns projects into reality.

ERS is operating and growing in one of the most complex multi-stakeholder environments in Canada — healthcare, construction, government, First Nations, real estate, and capital markets all converge in this company. This role sits at the center of all of them.

The Strategic Mandate

Public Relations & Engagement

ERS has relationships with health authorities, construction companies, unions, Indigenous communities, municipalities, and government bodies — but they are not yet synchronized. Each of those relationships is being developed independently, approached differently, and communicated to in isolation.

This role changes that. The mandate is to build a unified, coordinated engagement strategy that brings every stakeholder category into a single, aligned campaign under the ERS brand — so that health authorities, construction unions, First Nations, government bodies, and associations are all hearing the same story, seeing the same positioning, and being guided toward the same outcome: ERS as the preferred workforce housing partner in BC and across Canada.

  • Running synchronized engagement campaigns across all sectors simultaneously
  • Managing all media — digital, print, and television — as one integrated program
  • Representing ERS at events, in boardrooms, on panels, and in the press
  • Building the reputation that precedes every deal

Finance

ERS is scaling from an operating company into a development company. That means capital: construction mortgages, investor equity, grants, government funds, development financing, and structured partnership deals. Right now those pieces are moving independently.

This role brings it all under one roof. The mandate is to build and manage the full financial stack — from the investor pitch to the construction loan to the grant submission — so that every project ERS pursues is properly capitalized and every financial opportunity is captured.

The Role — Responsibilities & Scope

Part A: Public Relations & Strategic Engagement

Stakeholder Sectors

🏗 Construction Unions & Labour Organizations
  • BC Building Trades, local union halls, project labour agreement stakeholders
  • Build and maintain relationships with union leadership across BC
  • Position ERS as the preferred housing partner for union-represented workers on major infrastructure projects
  • Work toward ERS housing becoming a negotiated benefit in project labour agreements
  • Support union members with stable, high-quality accommodations that improve retention and morale on long-duration projects
🩺 Healthcare Unions
  • Nurses unions, healthcare workers unions, CUPE health divisions
  • Engage on the housing-as-retention argument: ERS housing directly reduces burnout, isolation, and turnover for travelling and rotating healthcare workers
  • Build ERS's profile as a champion of frontline healthcare worker wellbeing
🏥 Healthcare Professional Associations
  • Doctors of BC
  • Canadian Medical Association (CMA)
  • BC College of Registered Nurses (BCCNR)
  • Other provincial and national healthcare professional bodies
  • These associations have direct reach into the healthcare workforce — building relationships here creates referral pipelines, partnership opportunities, and credibility that opens doors at the health authority level
🏛 Government & Infrastructure Bodies
  • Infrastructure BC — active engagement on UHNBC Prince George and future provincially funded projects
  • BC Housing and relevant provincial ministries
  • Federal government bodies (CMHC, NRCAN, health-related federal programs)
  • Goal: preferred partner designation on major publicly funded projects
🔨 Industry Associations — Construction & Real Estate
  • Vancouver Regional Construction Association (VRCA)
  • BC Construction Association (BCCA)
  • BC Road Builders & Heavy Construction Association (BCCR)
  • Urban Land Institute and real estate development associations
  • These relationships build ERS's credibility with general contractors and project owners who need housing solutions for their crews
🌿 First Nations & Indigenous Communities
  • Treaty Nations and Band Councils adjacent to ERS project areas
  • Economic development arms of First Nations governments
  • Equity development partnerships — land, capital, and joint operations
  • Reconciliation-aligned housing programs that serve Indigenous healthcare and construction workers
  • Goal: 2–3 active First Nations equity development partnerships in Northern BC
🏥 Health Authorities
  • Northern Health, Interior Health, Vancouver Coastal Health, Fraser Health, Island Health
  • Primary goal: master lease agreements that give ERS institutional standing across a health region
  • Secondary goal: preferred vendor status for traveller, locum, and agency staff housing
🏙 Municipalities
  • Prince George, Dawson Creek, Terrace, Prince Rupert, Squamish, and others where ERS is active or expanding
  • Build relationships with mayors, economic development offices, and planning departments
  • Municipal support accelerates development permits and positions ERS as a community asset
  • Make the case: ERS housing reduces strain on local rental markets and supports hospital and contractor workforce stability

Engagement Campaigns

Five synchronized campaigns running at all times — Siavash builds, manages, and evolves each one under the unified ERS brand narrative.

  1. Healthcare Worker Housing Campaign — targeting health authorities and healthcare unions simultaneously; positions ERS as the solution to recruitment and retention
  2. Construction Workforce Campaign — timed to major project starts; targets union leadership and general contractors
  3. First Nations Development Campaign — community-first framing; equity, partnership, and shared outcomes
  4. Investor & Partner Awareness Campaign — building ERS credibility with private equity, mission-aligned capital, and strategic partners
  5. Municipal & Government Campaign — making the case to city councils, economic development offices, and provincial stakeholders

Events & Industry Presence

  • Identify and secure ERS presence at 6–10 key industry events per year across healthcare, construction, real estate, and government sectors
  • Build a speaking circuit for Austin — panels, keynotes, roundtables
  • Host ERS-branded roundtables and information sessions with targeted stakeholder groups
  • Represent ERS at events where Austin cannot be present

Part B: Finance

Investor Relations & Capital Raising

  • Build and maintain all investor-facing materials: pitch decks, one-pagers, financial summaries, impact reports
  • Manage the full investor pipeline — from first conversation to term sheet to close
  • Structure ERS's investment offering: equity, preferred returns, debt instruments, hybrid structures — matched to investor type and project needs
  • Develop the investor community: physicians, private equity, real estate investors, family offices, mission-aligned capital
  • Ontario opportunity: Active partner in place, new hospital project opening — requires structured outreach, deal framing, and investment proposal

Development Finance

  • Lead financial modeling for all development projects:
    • Dawson Creek Hub — development permit stage, construction financing required
    • UHNBC Prince George — housing supply model, development costs, operating revenue, revenue sharing
    • Ontario project — investment structure, partner terms, returns model
  • Build project-level pro formas: land, construction, operating revenue, debt service, investor returns
  • Prepare complete financing packages for lenders

Lender Relationships

  • Lakeview Credit Union — active mortgage relationship; advance to construction mortgage stage
  • Identify and engage additional lenders (credit unions, alternative lenders, CMHC programs) as the project pipeline grows
  • Maintain clean, current financial packages for all active projects so lenders can move quickly

Grants & Government Funds

  • Research, write, and submit all grant and funding applications
  • Active files:
    • Peace Community Trust — in progress; manage and advance
    • Offset Construction Challenge — applied; track and respond
  • Build a grants calendar — track every applicable funding program, cycle, and deadline across BC Housing, CMHC, NRCAN, First Nations funds, federal infrastructure programs, and others
  • Manage awarded funds: reporting requirements, milestone disbursements, compliance documentation

Budgeting & Financial Models

  • Build and maintain ERS's operating budget
  • Create financial models for each development project, each engagement campaign, and each client contract
  • Campaign budgeting as a deal line item: PR and engagement costs must be built into each project proposal — not treated as overhead. Every deal structure includes a media and stakeholder engagement budget.

Partnership Finance

  • Build financial proposals and deal structures for strategic partnerships: equity developers (JV), master lease partners, government co-investment
  • Draft term sheet frameworks and partnership agreements in coordination with legal
