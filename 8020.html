⚡ The 80/20 Analysis
The 20% That Drives 80% of the Outcome
Four moves. Executed well, they unlock everything else.
1
Sign One Master Lease Agreement with Northern Health
💰 Revenue Impact: $68M–$113M/year at scale
Northern Health has 18 hospitals, 10,000+ staff, 20–30% traveling at any time — ~3,000 housing placements needed. Their current housing is ad-hoc, fragmented, and explicitly identified as a “barrier to recruitment.” They NEED a partner. ERS’s $105/night blended model is exactly what they’re building toward.
What “signed” looks like:
  • Tier 1: Operating partnership / master service agreement (housing provider of record)
  • Northern Health commits to filling units, ERS manages and operates
  • Single invoice. Standardized operations. Performance-indexed.
The board-level case:
  • Reduces housing volatility for NH workforce
  • Improves staff retention (quality accommodations = nurses “look forward to coming back”)
  • Shifts housing from reactive admin to strategic asset
  • Capital-light for NH (they don’t build, ERS does)
  • Phased rollout — no overcommitment upfront
Current entry point: Bjorn Petersen (Regional Manager, Housing & Childcare BD). This is the door.
2
Close One GC Contract (EllisDon / PCL / Bird)
💰 Revenue Impact: $2M–$10M per project contract
Large GCs on hospital builds have 300–1,800 workers per project, 10–50% needing housing. They currently solve this with fragmented hotel blocks. ERS offers a single contract, managed housing, done.
The UHNBC opportunity (Prince George, EllisDon, $1.58B):
  • 400 avg daily workers, 800 peak — 30% needing housing = 120–240 beds needed
  • Construction runs 2026–2030 | Housing spend: $37M | ERS capture at 30% = ~$11M
Target: EllisDon BC — (604) 630-2500. Ask for the UHNBC project lead.
Opening Line

“We manage workforce housing for healthcare construction projects across BC — we already work with Northern Health. With the UHNBC tower mobilizing, I wanted to introduce ERS before the housing gap becomes a problem. Can I send a one-pager?”

3
Lock In the Dawson Creek Hub (Phase 1 Build)
💰 Revenue Impact: $1.3M–$1.8M/year per hub
Austin’s land at 10620 10th St, Dawson Creek is the physical proof of concept for everything. 50% of Dawson Creek hospital construction workers are from out of town. This is the model that gets replicated at 5 hub locations.
The numbers:
  • ~32–40 purpose-built units | Development cost: ~$16M
  • Monthly income: $110K (residential) → $150K (mixed-use)
  • Financing: CMHC + BC Housing + private equity + Northern Health master lease as anchor
When NH sees a finished hub in Dawson Creek, the conversation shifts from “can you do this?” to “how fast can you scale?”
4
Get in Front of One Board in 60 Days
💰 Revenue Impact: First Board approval = platform for all subsequent deals
A Board resolution from one health authority changes ERS’s positioning permanently. You go from “startup” to “approved vendor.” Every other health authority then has a reference they can point to.
What a Health Authority Board needs to see:
  • The problem statement (housing = #1 recruitment barrier — you have the data)
  • The proven model (34 units, 6 hospitals, $105/night, zero vacancy)
  • The partnership structure (4-tier capital stack, phased rollout)
  • The risk mitigation (they don’t need to commit capital — ERS carries it)
Path: Bjorn Petersen → David Williams (VP HR) → Northern Health CEO → board presentation request

ERS — The Case in 5 Minutes

The Problem

60% of Canadian healthcare workers are burned out. 80% feel isolated. The #1 barrier to rural healthcare recruitment is housing — Northern Health has explicitly said so.

The Market

$600–800M/year in Canada. $60M/year in BC alone. No dedicated specialized provider exists. The market is served through fragmented, expensive, ad-hoc arrangements.

ERS’s Position

4 years incorporated. 34 units managed. 6 hospital partnerships. $105/night (vs. $125–$200 market rate). 24/7 support. Single invoice.

What We’re Building

The full-stack infrastructure layer for healthcare workforce housing in Canada. Starting with Northern BC — highest need, lowest competition, highest percentage of traveling staff. Purpose-built hubs anchored by master lease agreements with health authorities and GC housing contracts on hospital builds.

The Ask

A master service agreement with [Northern Health / your organization] that makes ERS the preferred housing provider for your traveling and temporary workforce. We carry the capital, manage the operations, deliver the reporting. You get housed staff, better retention, and one invoice.

The Proof

Dawson Creek Hub development at 10620 10th St — first purpose-built healthcare housing hub in Northern BC. Phase 1 ready for co-design.
